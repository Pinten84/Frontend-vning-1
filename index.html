Swedish Chicken Stew (Kycklinggryta)

This is my absolute favorite comfort food recipe that my grandmother taught me. This creamy, hearty chicken stew is perfect for cold winter days and brings back wonderful childhood memories of family dinners. The rich flavors and tender chicken make it a dish that everyone loves!

Ingredients

  • 1 kg (2.2 lbs) chicken thighs, boneless and skinless, cut into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 carrots, sliced
  • 3 celery stalks, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 500ml (2 cups) chicken stock
  • 200ml (3/4 cup) heavy cream
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • 300g (10 oz) small potatoes, halved

Cooking Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
  2. Season chicken chunks with salt and pepper, then brown them on all sides (about 5-6 minutes). Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add diced onion and cook until softened, about 3 minutes.
  4. Add carrots, celery, and garlic. Cook for another 3-4 minutes until vegetables start to soften.
  5. Sprinkle flour over the vegetables and stir well. Cook for 1 minute to remove raw flour taste.
  6. Gradually add chicken stock while stirring constantly to prevent lumps.
  7. Add bay leaves, thyme, paprika, and halved potatoes. Bring to a boil.
  8. Return the browned chicken to the pot and reduce heat to low. Simmer covered for 25-30 minutes.
  9. Stir in heavy cream and simmer for an additional 5 minutes until sauce thickens.
  10. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.
  11. Remove bay leaves and garnish with fresh chopped parsley before serving.
  12. Serve hot with crusty bread or lingonberry sauce on the side.

Perfect Pairings

This hearty stew pairs wonderfully with warm open-faced sandwiches (varmamackor) and a simple side salad. The combination makes for a complete and satisfying Swedish meal!
